Living in Alaska

(And why everyone should at some point)



I lived in Alaska for a year and a half (yes it unfortunately covered 2 winters), and I can truly say that there is nothing like this state. It is incredibly beautiful year round, the people are amazing, the church is strong, and there is something powerful about the intensity of the state.

If you are considering moving there, even for a year or two to experience something new, here is how I would rank different cities:



  1. Palmer
    • Mat-Su Valley
  2. Fairbanks
    • Interior Alaska
  3. Eagle River
    • Just North of Anchorage
  4. Valdez
    • Prince William Sound
  5. Juneau
    • South-East
  6. Soldotna
    • Kenai Peninsula
  7. North Pole
    • Interior Alaska
  8. Anchorage
    • Biggest City Alaska Has
  9. Ketchikan
    • South-East
  10. Wasilla
    • Mat-Su Valley


Palmer

Palmer is a small town about an hour north of Anchorage. This makes it still fairly convienent to get to the city where there is an international airport, costco, and temple. Its also only 20-30 minutes away from Wasilla, which is much bigger and has a good selection of restauraunts and shopping. Palmer is right along the Matanuska River and is nestled within a mountain range. It is beautiful, and is ideal for any summer fishing, hiking, and exploring. On the downside, it is pretty windy, and the windchill creates some brutal winter temperatures.



Fairbanks

Fairbanks is far more intense from a weather standpoint than Palmer. In the summer temperatures can reach 90 degrees, and in the winter you can see temperatures as low as 50 below. I lived there in a pretty intense winter and saw -54. Fairbanks also has an airport, although it is reletively small, and mostly connects through Anchorage or Seattle. There will also soon be a temple built there which is really exciting. There aren't as many mountains nearby, but there are hot springs about an hour down the road, and in the winter you can ice fish and snow machine all you want (when it's warm enough)!




Eagle River

Eagle River is almost a suburb of Anchorage, only being about 30 minutes away from most of the city. It is a beautiful and faily affluent area, and keeps you outside the crazy of Anchorage. Because it is a very mountain-y town, there is not much sun that gets over the mountians in the winter. This is one of the nicest areas in Alaska though.




Valdez

Valdez is a tiny little town on the Prince William Sound. It is home to about 3,000 people although that almost doubles in the summer for fishing. It is one of the best places in Alaska to fish for salmon (which is really saying something). This is also where the oil pipeline ends (remember the Exxon crisis, happened in Valdez). Living here you are 4 hours away from Palmer, and 5 hours from Anchorage with nothing much in between. The drive is beautiful though, passing by both the Matanuska and Worthington Glaciers. Unfortunately, the pass to leaving Valdez can close in the winter for weeks because of snow, resticting access. Valdez is also the snow capital of America with the highest average snowfall each year.




Juneau

Juneau is the capital of Alaksa, even though its on an Island inaccessible except by plane or boat. It is also coincidentally the home to the world's smallest Costco! Juneau is breathtakingly beautiful in the summer, as it is a rainforest. The few sunny days you get though make it worthwhile. There are so many hikes, so much fishing, and so many tourists in the summer. One must see is the Mendenhall Glacier. One of the most famous glaciers, this huge piece of ice is a major part of the landscape.




Soldotna

Soldotna is on the Kenai Peninsula, the fishing capital of Alaska. You can easily fish for any of the 5 kinds of salmon found in Alaska (depending on what is running at the time), as well as halibut, rockfish, and anything else you could desire. It is fairly remote, with only one road that can get you back to Anchorage. Often traveling to and from Soldotna means riding on a bush plane.




North Pole

North Pole is actually just slightly south of Fairbanks, about a thirty minute drive (depending on the roads). Santa does in fact have a house here, and all the street lights look like candy canes. Its a very small town though, and there isn't much to do without going to Fairbanks.




Anchorage

Anchorage is the biggest city in Alaska, although its still smaller than your average 'small city' in Texas (which is coincidentally less than half the size of Alaska). Anchorage has a larger airport, temple, and Costco. It is also one of the 10 most dangerous cities in America, which is unofortunate, however msot of the problems are in North Anchorage, so if you stay South, you should be fine. Anchorage is close to so many beautiful hikes and adventures though, so you shouldn't count it out. It also has a very impressive system of walking trails, making it a remakably bikeable city.




Ketchikan

Ketchikan is a tiny town on a little island in South-East Alaksa. It is beautiful, but very remote. It is also a very common stop for cruise ships as they cruise along the inside passage.




Wasilla

Wasilla is a very small city just west of Palmer. It has a pretty decent selection of things to do, places to shop, and nature to explore. It is really windy though basically all the time which is annoying in the summer, and brutal in the winter. It isn't unheard of for them to have power outtages and roofs come off due to windstorms.
